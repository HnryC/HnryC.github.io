Characteristics of metals

  • Good conductors of heat and electricity
  • Lustrous: Shiny when freshly cut/polished
  • Malleable: Can be bent/squashed into different shapes
  • Ductile: Can be stretched into thin wires

Metals and their uses

Metal Use Reason
Aluminium (Al) Power liens wires, saucepans, cans, foil Conductor of heat/electricity, ductile, light, non-toxic
Copper (Cu) Electrical wiring Ductile, conductor of electricity
Lead (Pb) Flashing (window waterproofing) Soft, malleable, high corrosion resistance
Mercury (Hg) Thermometers Liquid at room temperature, expands rapidly with heat
Sodium (Na) Nuclear reactant coolant Conducts heat well, melts at low temperature to allow it to be pushed through pipes
Tin (Sn) Steel food can coatings Non-toxic, doesn't react with food, stops rust
Zinc (Zn) Coating for iron/steel Zinc is more reactive than iron and steel, and zinc oxide prevents iron's exposer to oxidisers

What are alloys?

  • Alloys are a combination of a pure metal with small amounts of other elements
  • These additives improve certain aspects of the metal and makes it better for human consumption
  • Examples of this include:
    • Carbon added to iron to create steel
    • Chromium and nickel added to steel to create stainless steel
    • Lead additives to tin to lower the melting point in soft solder
